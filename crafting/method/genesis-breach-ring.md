# Genesis Breach Ring

## Goal

Use the Genesis Tree inside the Monastery of the Keepers to craft a high-quality Breach Ring with a catalyst-boosted, tag-biased set of modifiers, producing a best-in-slot or near-BiS ring that sells to endgame players willing to pay premium for optimised jewellery.

## Target base(s)

Breach Ring bases (any sub-type). These are the only ring bases that accept up to 50% quality from catalysts (vs 20% cap on standard rings), and with Genesis Tree nodes can reach 65% quality — multiplicatively boosting all catalyst-tagged modifier values. See ../../knowledge/sources.md (poe2db.tw) for the full list of Breach Ring sub-types and their implicit modifiers.

## Starting capital

**Tier:** high

Requires Atlas endgame access (Monastery of the Keepers), a meaningful Genesis Tree investment (Hive Blood + Wombgifts from Breaches to allocate the relevant nodes), a stock of catalyst currency of the correct tag type, and Exalted Orbs / Perfect Exalted Orbs for final affix slams. A single attempt requires 30–60 catalyst applications (each catalyst is individually scarce — obtained only from Genesis Tree crafts) plus 3–6 Greater or Perfect Exalted Orbs. Total currency floor is on the order of 200–500 Exalted Orbs before accounting for base acquisition.

## Recipe

1. Allocate Genesis Tree nodes targeting:
   - The **ring branch** (Breach Ring birthing, up to 50% base quality).
   - The **currency branch** containing the "Riches Beget Riches" node if available (chance for bonus Exalted Orbs per craft).
   - The catalyst type matching your target mod tags (e.g., Flesh catalyst branch for life mods, Reaver for attack mods, Sibilant for caster/spell mods).
2. Acquire Hive Blood (from Hiveborn kills) and Wombgifts (from Marshals in stabilised Breaches) to fund tree node allocation.
3. Birth a Breach Ring via the Genesis Tree ring branch. The tree may guarantee a base modifier tier floor and unlock exclusive Breach-only ring sub-types.
4. Apply **10–20 Catalysts** of the target type to the ring before any Exalt slams. Catalysts raise quality up to 50% (or 65% with tree nodes), multiplicatively boosting all mods sharing the catalyst tag. Apply catalysts first — catalyst bias on Exalt slams requires quality to already be present.
5. Activate an **Omen of Catalysing Exaltation** (biases subsequent Exalted Orb slam toward mods sharing the catalyst tag, roughly 5× weight at 20% quality; higher at 40–65%).
6. Slam a **Greater Exalted Orb** to add a tag-biased mod. Inspect. If the mod is on target, proceed; if not, use Annulment + omen to remove it and retry.
7. Repeat steps 5–6 for each open slot (rings have up to 6 affixes), using side-targeting Omens (Sinistral/Dextral Exaltation) to distribute mods between prefixes and suffixes.
8. Once 4–6 mods are in place, apply additional catalysts up to the quality cap to maximise numeric values of all catalyst-tagged mods.
9. Apply **Divine Orbs** to maximise rolls on non-tagged mods. Use Omen of Sanctification + Divine only if the ring is at near-mirror quality (sanctified rolls lock out most further crafting).
10. List on trade. Consider corruption only at full quality with all mods finalized — Vaal is irreversible.

## Outcome odds

Each Exalted Orb slam under Omen of Catalysing Exaltation at 20% quality draws from a pool where catalyst-tagged mods have approximately 5× the weight of non-tagged mods. At 40% quality the bias is roughly 7.5×. Absolute odds depend on the number of catalyst-tagged mods in the ilvl 80+ ring pool for that Breachlord variant — typically 8–15 tag-appropriate mods out of a full pool of 40–60 entries. With 5× bias, effectively 40–75 weighted units out of a biased-pool total, giving a rough hit-rate per slam on any catalyst-tag mod of 40–60%. Exact per-mod weights are community-approximated; see craftofexile.com via ../../knowledge/sources.md for current figures. Genesis Tree node unlocks may further restrict or guarantee specific mods — check in-game node descriptions before investing.

## Inputs

- Hive Blood + Wombgifts (farmed from Breach encounters) to fund Genesis Tree node allocation
- ~10–30 Catalysts per ring (type depends on target mod — Flesh for life, Reaver for attack, Sibilant for caster/spell, etc.) per session
- ~3–6 Greater or Perfect Exalted Orbs per ring attempt
- ~2–4 Omen of Catalysing Exaltation per ring
- ~2–4 Sinistral / Dextral Exaltation Omens for side-targeting
- ~1–2 Divine Orbs for numeric value perfection
- Optional: 1 Vaal Orb for corruption gamble

## Risk / variance

High. Genesis Tree node investment is a sunk cost that pays off only across many crafts, not a single attempt. Individual ring crafts can produce 2–3 useful tag-appropriate mods plus 1–2 random mods that lower the value significantly. Annulling bad mods risks removing a good mod on an unprotected side (Fracture + Annul isolation is expensive at this tier). Divine Orb rolls can push a 4-mod ring to sub-optimal values. The method is profitable in expectation over a session of 10+ rings, not guaranteed on any single ring. Expect ~30–40% of outputs to be vendor fodder and ~60–70% to be listable at varying price points.

## Why it sells

Breach Rings are the only rings in PoE2 that can reach 50–65% quality, multiplicatively boosting all matching mod values. A Breach Ring with 50%+ quality and three catalyst-biased mods (e.g., 65% quality Flesh-catalyst ring with T1 maximum life on two prefixes and a life-recovery suffix) represents a permanent power ceiling above what a standard ring can reach. BiS-seeking players in endgame optimisation mode pay 5–50+ Divines for rings that complete their defensive or offensive stacks.

## Worked example

> Runes of Aldur, patch 0.5.3, 2026-06-22 (illustrative)
>
> Target: Flesh-catalyst Breach Ring, 50% quality, T1 flat life + T1 max life + chaos resist + life regen  
> Inputs: ~25 Flesh Catalysts + 4 Greater Exalted Orbs + 3 Catalysing Exaltation Omens + 2 Sinistral/Dextral Omens + 1 Divine Orb  
> Estimated currency cost: ~350 exalts ≈ ~3 div  
> Result after 2 rings attempted: 1 vendor fodder ring, 1 listable T1-life + chaos resist + decent third mod ring  
> Expected sale: ~6–12 div for the quality ring with two T1 mods  
> Margin:        ~3–9 div per successful craft across two attempts
>
> Note: Catalyst supply is tightly controlled by Genesis Tree node availability per account — factor this as an opportunity cost, not just a price. Breach Ring prices collapse as league matures and supply rises; best window is weeks 1–3.
