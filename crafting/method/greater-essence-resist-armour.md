# Greater Essence Resist Armour

## Goal

Apply a Greater Essence to a high-ilvl armour base to lock in a guaranteed resistance mod, then fill open affix slots with Exalted Orbs to produce a mid-tier rare armour piece that sells to players lacking resistances.

## Target base(s)

Any ilvl 75+ body armour, gloves, or boots with a high Energy Shield or Armour roll. Prioritise ilvl 81–82 to unlock the top modifier tier pools. See ../../knowledge/sources.md for tier data on poe2db.tw.

## Starting capital

**Tier:** ssf

No tradeable currency required. Essences drop naturally in acts and early maps; the method works with whatever Greater Essence you accumulate. A typical run needs ~4–8 Greater Essences of any resist-aligned type plus 2–4 Exalted Orbs to fill open slots. The base item can be self-found.

## Recipe

1. Identify a self-found ilvl 75+ armour base (body armour, gloves, or boots) that is Normal (white) or Magic (blue) with no crafted modifier already occupying its crafted slot.
2. If the base is Magic with an existing crafted mod, use an Orb of Annulment to clear the crafted mod first (or start from a fresh Normal base via vendor recipe).
3. Apply a Greater Essence whose guaranteed modifier matches what you need most (e.g., Greater Essence of Loathing for chaos resistance; resist-type essences for elemental resists). The item upgrades to Rare with the guaranteed mod locked in.
4. Inspect the resulting item. If the essence mod landed on a prefix slot, use an Exalted Orb targeting a suffix to add a second mod; if it landed on a suffix, target the prefix side. (Pair with a Sinistral or Dextral Exaltation omen if available, to force the slam onto the correct side.)
5. Repeat Exalted slams until the item has 4–5 mods total, or until you hit a sellable combination (two resists + life, or ES + resist).
6. If a bad mod appears and you have a spare Chaos Orb, use Omen of Whittling + Chaos Orb to replace the lowest-ilvl mod rather than bricking with a blind Chaos slam.
7. List on trade or equip; repeat.

## Outcome odds

Three-to-five affix rolls from a pool of ~30–50 mods at ilvl 75+. The guaranteed essence mod is always present; each subsequent Exalted Orb slam draws randomly from the remaining open pool. With 4 mods on an armour piece there are roughly 12–18 resist/life mods competing with ~20+ non-resists, giving an order-of-magnitude estimate of ~30–40% per slam for a useful resist or life mod. No craftofexile.com tool-sourced precise figure is available for this exact slot/tier combination — see ../../knowledge/sources.md to query craftofexile.com directly for current per-mod weights on your target base.

## Inputs

- ~4–8 Greater Essences (any resist-aligned type: Loathing, Envy, Contempt, etc.)
- ~2–4 Exalted Orbs per attempt (for filling open affix slots)
- 1 Normal or Magic armour base (self-found)
- Optional: 1–2 Orbs of Annulment if base has a conflicting crafted mod
- Optional: 1 Sinistral/Dextral Exaltation Omen per slam for side-targeting

## Risk / variance

Low. The essence mod is guaranteed; the only variance is in the 2–3 additional mods added by Exalted Orbs. A "failed" craft is not bricked — you can still sell an armour with one good resist at a reduced price (~3–10 Exalted Orbs to another player). Chance of hitting a two-resist + life result in ≤3 slams: qualitatively low (~10–20% estimate). Expect to use multiple essences across several bases before landing a clean two-resist + life piece. The method is forgiving because even near-misses are vendor-able.

## Why it sells

Resistances are the primary gearing bottleneck for new and progression characters throughout the atlas. Players switching builds or entering the endgame routinely pay a premium for armour pieces that patch their resistance holes without requiring a mirror-level investment. An armour with guaranteed chaos resist (from Essence of Loathing) is especially desirable because chaos resist appears on fewer base mods than elemental resists.

> Inputs used: 6 Greater Essences of Loathing (chaos resist) + 3 Exalted Orbs across two attempts  
> Attempt 1 result: chaos resist + armour + life — listed and sold  
> Input cost:    ~0.05 div (6 essences + 3 exalts at ~100–130 exalts/div)  
> Expected sale: ~0.3–1 div (chaos resist + life armour, mid tier)  
> Margin:        ~0.25–0.95 div per successful attempt (before failed attempts)
>
