# Transmute-Augment-Regal Weapon Flip

## Goal

Cycle white weapon bases through the Transmutation → Augmentation → Regal chain to produce Magic or Rare weapons with two relevant attack modifiers, then sell them to low-budget players who cannot afford crafted endgame gear.

## Target base(s)

Any ilvl 60–75 martial weapon base (quarterstaff, sword, axe, mace) with a high implicit physical damage roll. Quarterstaves and bows work especially well because their mod pools include high-value added damage and attack speed suffixes. See ../../knowledge/sources.md for current per-base mod pools on poe2db.tw.

## Starting capital

**Tier:** low

Requires only common currency orbs. A session budget of ~20–50 Orbs of Transmutation and ~10–20 Orbs of Augmentation and ~5–10 Regal Orbs is sufficient to run several dozen attempts and find multiple sellable items. All three orb types are among the most common currency drops in the campaign and early maps.

## Recipe

1. Acquire white (Normal) weapon bases at ilvl 60+ by farming maps at the appropriate tier or buying raw bases cheaply (1–3 Exalted Orbs each from other players selling bulk drops).
2. Apply an Orb of Transmutation to upgrade the base to Magic with one random modifier.
3. Inspect the modifier. If the mod is useful (added physical damage, attack speed, critical strike chance, elemental damage), proceed. If it is bad (reduced attack speed, reflects damage), apply an Orb of Alteration to reroll the Magic item, or discard and transmute a new base.
4. Apply an Orb of Augmentation to add a second Magic modifier. Inspect both mods.
5. If both mods are useful (e.g., flat physical + attack speed, or two elemental damage bonuses), the item is potentially sellable as-is at a Magic price point — list it.
6. If the two-mod combination is excellent, apply a Regal Orb to upgrade to Rare (adds one random mod). Inspect the Rare. If the result has two strong mods plus the new Regal mod, list as a Rare weapon at a higher price point.
7. If the Rare is mediocre, use an Exalted Orb to add one more mod. Stop when you have 3–4 mods and the item is listable.
8. Discard or vendor items with zero useful mods to recover partial value.

## Outcome odds

Each Transmutation pulls from the full Magic modifier pool for that base and ilvl. For a typical ilvl 65 quarterstaff, the pool contains roughly 40–60 distinct modifiers; attack-useful mods (flat phys, added elemental, attack speed, crit) account for perhaps 30–40% of weight. The chance of both Transmutation AND Augmentation landing on useful mods is approximately 10–20% per pair — a rough estimate based on observed PoE2 mod pool sizes; verify current exact weights on craftofexile.com (see ../../knowledge/sources.md). The Regal Orb adds a third random mod from the remaining Rare pool, with similar per-mod odds.

## Inputs

- ~1 Orb of Transmutation per attempt
- ~1 Orb of Augmentation per attempt
- ~1 Regal Orb per promoted attempt (only on good Magic items)
- ~1–2 Orbs of Alteration per failed Magic reroll
- Optionally: 1–2 Exalted Orbs to fill an extra affix slot on promising Rares
- White weapon bases (self-found or cheaply bought in bulk)

## Risk / variance

Very low per-attempt loss. A failed Transmutation costs one Transmutation Orb; a failed Regal costs one Regal Orb. Neither loss is painful individually. The method is inherently high-volume: you might run 30–50 Transmutation cycles to find 2–4 sellable items. The primary cost is time, not currency. Variance is in whether the final Regal adds a useful or useless mod — adding a bad mod to an already-good Magic item wastes the Regal Orb but the item may still be sellable as a two-mod rare at a discount.

## Why it sells

Low-level and mid-tier players constantly need weapon upgrades during acts 1–3 and early maps. A Magic two-mod weapon with flat physical damage plus attack speed represents a genuine upgrade over self-found gear, and buyers at this stage cannot yet afford essence or omen crafts. The price point (5–30 Exalted Orbs) is the sweet spot where demand is high and listing volume is moderate.

>

> Gross proceeds: ~0.75–1.25 div for the session  
> Margin:        ~0.25–0.75 div per session, before failed attempts discarded
